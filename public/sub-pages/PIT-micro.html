UPDATE: As of late September, Bill 1414 was deemed unconstitutional by a Florida Circuit Court Judge, and the ban on Tegus and Iguanas was lifted. However, FWC is still planning on sweeping changes to the rules on possession of non-native conditional and non-conditional species and will be meeting in February 2021 to discuss rule changes. It is our STRONG recommendation that you keep aware of these upcoming changes to FWC rules, and get your reptiles chipped in advance of these rules. We are now offering our microchipping services to all reptiles and exotic animals that are able to be chipped for the same $25 cost that we originally offered only to Tegus and Green Iguanas. Please contact us for an appointment today!

We are keeping the information below here so you can see a history of why we began offering this service:

On July 1, 2020, the Florida State Senate Bill 1414 was enacted into law as Executive order 20-19, effectively banning the possession and trade of Green Iguanas (Iguana iguana and all morphs of this species) and Tegus (all species in the genera Salvator and Tupinambis). As such, the Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission (also known as FWC) is requiring that all individuals or entities that were in possession of these species not already grandfathered in are to apply to be able to keep their pets. The FWC website says: "Per EO 20-19, individuals who had a pet green iguana or tegu prior to July 1, 2020 can apply for a Prohibited Species for Personal Use Permit to lawfully possess that animal for the remainder of its life."

In short: If you owned a pet green iguana or tegu BEFORE July 1, 2020, you MUST apply for this permit to continually legally possess your pet. If you want to obtain a Tegu or Green Iguana (or any morph of these) after July 1, 2020, it is now illegal to obtain one in the State of Florida by ANY means.

If you have any question as to whether you were automatically grandfathered in, you most likely were NOT grandfathered in... only 150 business entities that held a Class III permit were given this status. Our Rescue was not even grandfathered in, and we are having to apply for a pet permit just like you.

Additionally, the new rules require each animal affected by the ban be PIT tagged BEFORE submitting this form.

We know that as responsible pet owners, this changing of the law may be adversely effecting you financially, especially during the COVID-19 pandemic. According to many of our friends, we hear that some veterinarians are asking as much as $300 PER Iguana or Tegu to provide PIT tagging!

Lauren Bertke, our rescue President, was a Veterinary Technician for 20 years, and has experience giving PIT tags to various mammals and reptiles, so when she heard of the requirement, she authorized the rescue to procure PIT tags from 911 PetChip and we are pleased to offer PIT tagging to all those affected by the new FWC requirements for only $25 per animal! We use "TinyChips" using a small 16 gauge needle, so even your young animals can be tagged. The $25 price even includes FREE Lifetime Registration though FreePetChipRegistry.com! Any proceeds from tagging will go directly into procuring as many chips as are needed.
