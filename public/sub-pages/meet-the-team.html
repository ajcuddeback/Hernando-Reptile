Meet the Team

Lauren Morello

President/Founder

Lauren Morello is originally from Long Island, NY. Her passion for rescuing animals in need began at a very young age. At the age of 6 she rescued her first pet, a Dutch bunny named Thumper. He was the first of what would eventually turn into thousands of rescues of animals of every size, shape, color, and species. As a teenager, Lauren worked as a veterinary assistant in an all-species day practice, as well as a veterinary technician at the North Shore Animal League America, the world’s largest no-kill animal rescue organization. By the time she was 16, she had her own rescued colony of 6 bunnies, a cat who had been abandoned at just two days old, a Patterdale terrier that had produced nearly a dozen litters for the puppy mill she was originally owned by until they had no more use for her and left her for dead, several ferrets, some guinea pigs, a hedgehog, and a beautiful American Saddlebred that was once bound for a Mexican slaughterhouse.

At 17 she, along with her horse Mr. Beans, left home to attend the University of Findlay as a double major in both English Equestrian studies and Pre-veterinary Medicine. During her freshman year, as part of her animal handling class, Lauren raised a Holstein/Guernsey steer which she ultimately purchased to avoid having him go to market. While in college, she opened her home to several unwanted dogs, pocket pet, and even her first pot belly pig. Although a drunk driver cut her college career short during her junior year, she continued to perform animal rescues throughout the three years she was in school and well beyond. After leaving school, she purchased a farm in Hagerstown, IN and took in dozens of unwanted dogs, cats, horses, pigs, and chickens.

A year later she relocated to Florida where the rescue efforts continued. Lauren started out as a foster home housing as many as 19 American pit bull terriers at one time. Since then, she helped run a dog and cat rescue in the Tampa Bay area, held jobs with Pasco County and the Florida Highway Patrol saving lives as a dispatcher, received acclaim from Cesar Millan (“The Dog Whisperer”) for her work with a special needs dog named Xander (https://www.cesarsway.com/live-in-the-moment), finally establishing Hernando Reptile and Exotics Rescue in 2019 which has received both Bronze and Silver awards in the Hernando Sun’s Readers Choice awards in 2019 and 2020, and obtained the prestigious Gold Award in 2022. Lauren also is a member of the Hernando County Fire & Emergency Services Venom Response Unit, helping to remove venomous reptiles and other wildlife from dangerous situations.

Joseph Colao

Vice President/Co-Founder

Joseph is originally from Selden, NY and moved to Florida in the winter of 2004. His love and passion for animals of all kinds started at a very young age. He was constantly in trouble for bringing home stray dogs, cats, and even wild birds! As a former president and vice-president of two local dog rescues, Joseph was honored to help Lauren create what is now Hernando Reptile and Exotics Rescue and work with “some of the best people in rescue and my favorite cold and warm-blooded animals.” His work history has always been about helping – working for the Middle Country School District and Independent Group Home Living (IGHL) back on Long Island, NY. Joe can always be found at our events touting the largest reptiles we have around so everyone, young and old alike, can gain an education about these magnificent creatures!

Jesse Cuddeback

Secretary/Treasurer/Volunteer Coordinator

Jesse was elected to our Board in January of 2020, and took on a multitude of responsibilities… officially as Secretary/Treasurer, but also wearing the hats of Volunteer Coordinator and Social Media Lead, all while volunteering much time and energy taking care of the Rescue ambassadors and adoptables and giving tours to the public. Jesse’s background is in sales/marketing, food service management, security and is a recent Law Enforcement Academy graduate. He has volunteered with the Lyon County Sheriff’s Department’s Volunteers in Policing (NV), Boy Scouts of America (Cub Scout Den Leader), Girl Scouts of America (Parent Volunteer) and the Silverada Little League (Assist. Coach). Jesse also serves with Lauren as a member of the Hernando County Fire & Emergency Services Venom Response Unit, helping to remove venomous reptiles and other wildlife from dangerous situations.

Dan Groover

Director of Veterinary Care / Social Media Manager

Dan has been working with animals since she was 13 by starting at her hometown zoo, then continued at a wildlife rehabilitation hospital as well as a big cat sanctuary, all before stepping into the veterinary world. She graduated from FSU in 2014 and has 8 years of vet tech experience from general practice to emergency/critical care. For the last 2 years she has been focused on exotic animal medicine. She has extensive knowledge on proper husbandry/dietary needs for exotic animals and loves to share that knowledge with others.

Dan recently moved to the Tampa bay area with her fiance and all of their animals. She heard the call for volunteers, joined up and became immediately active in all aspects of the rescue, wherever she could help. Most importantly, Dan has been instrumental in providing the TLC needed for some very sick intakes, saving the lives of several neglected and special needs reptiles. She is excited to help the rescue expand and make sure the animals receive medical care and excellent nutrition during their stay and we are thrilled to have her in such an important role.
