Volunteer!

Do you have a passion for animals? So do we!!! Hernando Reptile and Exotics Rescue, Inc. is 100% run by volunteers, from the top down! Not one of our Board Members are paid, and, in fact, during slow financial times, our Board Members are the ones financially supporting the daily care of our residents.

As many of you may know, our rescue is run out of our home in a residential neighborhood in the middle of Spring Hill, Florida. In times of high population, you can't turn a corner without meeting several of our temporary residents or Ambassadors. â€‹Hernando Reptile and Exotics Rescue, Inc. is the brainchild of Lauren Bertke who realized that there was a desperate need to shelter unwanted reptiles and other non-native species in our area when she personally rescued "Cookie" in May of 2017 as well as "Mitch" and "Merida" in 2018, all who were Columbian Red Tailed Boas. At the end of 2018, three snakes (2 carpet pythons and one Bredli python) were given to her in a large Rubbermaid container with just pot of water after their temporary owner did everything they could to take care of them. What started out as 6 beautiful snakes became a multitude of over 100 animals, from snakes to tortoises, leopard geckos to a blue arctic fox, iguanas to tegus.

As you can imagine, running a rescue of over 100 animals in a 3 bedroom home can be a quite daunting task. Keeping it clean and smelling fresh is even more difficult. This is why we need you!!! We need people who will go the extra mile, aren't afraid of getting dirty, and will work hard to ensure the well being and safety of every one of our residents.

Because we are a registered 501(c)(3) non-profit organization, all volunteer hours performed here are accepted as credit for schools, scholarships and community service hours (we will accept volunteers for court-ordered non-violent offenders on a case-by-case basis). While we are looking for individuals 18 and older, we will accept students younger than 18 on occassion, but require parental permission.

Qualifications to become a Volunteer:

  • Be at least 18 years of age (unless given a special waiver with parental permission).
  • Have a clean background check (some exceptions will be given to those who need court-ordered community service hours).
  • Be willing to make a solid commitment (10 hours a month or more volunteer service in two or more days combined minimum).
  • Have transportation to and from the rescue.
  • Have a solid work ethic and are not afraid to get dirty! (Yes, you will have to clean animal pee and poop!)
  • Have a teamwork mentality and be a self starter!
  • Be Drama-Free and know how to have fun!
